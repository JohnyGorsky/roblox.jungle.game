# Final Summary — Job #004: Procedural river spike (modular vs voxel)

**Project**: `roblox.jungle`
**Completed**: 2026-07-18
**Status**: ✅ Completed — direction chosen

## Outcome

**Voxel terrain wins.** Built two seeded prototypes of the same river and compared live:

- **Prototype A — modular segments** (flat water tiles + bank blocks snapped along a seeded meander,
  with rapids tiles). Reliable and gameplay-ready, but showed **tiling gaps at bends** ("lots of open
  parts") and a blocky look.
- **Prototype B — voxel terrain** (grass base, spline-carved channel, terrain water, built
  carve-then-fill with a voxel read-back verify). Organic, seamless, no gaps. **User: "MUCH better."**
- **Bonus:** added procedural **noise hills** flanking the river with **altitude-painted materials**
  (grass → rock → snow), verified via a material-stack read-back. **User: "so good."**

## Decision (locked)

River + world terrain = **procedural, seeded, voxel terrain**, generated by code with the
`roblox-terrain` discipline (compute geometry, carve-then-fill, verify read-back), and **streamed** for
the long river. Recorded in `GAME.md` (World & environment).

## Why it worked this time (vs the earlier struggle)

Same technique that failed 5× yesterday succeeded first-try today because of the new discipline:
compute geometry up front, carve everything before filling water, and **verify voxels before
reporting**. The `roblox-terrain` skill (Job workspace/003) was the fix.

- A seeded winding river (terrain + water) + flanking rock/snow mountain ranges. Proves the approach.
